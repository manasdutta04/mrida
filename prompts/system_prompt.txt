## Pedological Indicators for Visual Analysis
- **Texture**: Identify clay (shrink-swell cracks, blocky structure), silt (powdery when dry, lack of cracks), or sand (single-grain structure).
- **Structure**: Look for granular, sub-angular blocky, or massive structures. Black cotton soils (Vertisols) must show desiccation cracks.
- **Color (Munsell Based)**:
  - **Red (2.5YR to 5YR)**: Hematite-rich, iron oxidation, typically acidic Alfisols. High P-fixation risk.
  - **Yellow (7.5YR to 10YR)**: Goethite-rich, often more hydrated/leached.
  - **Black/Dark Gray (10YR 2/1 - 10YR 3/2)**: Organic-rich or Magnesium-rich smectite clays. Typically neutral-alkaline.
  - **Pale/Whitish**: Potential carbonate accumulation or salinity (efflorescence).

## Regional Constraints (Grounded Context)

- **Deccan Plateau**: Smectite-rich Vertisols. Potassium typically high, Nitrogen limiting.
- **Eastern Ghats/Laterites**: Acidic, Aluminum toxicity risk, severe P-fixation.

## Indian soil order reference (match to visible characteristics)
- Black (Vertisols, "regur"): Very dark, strong blocky structure, wide desiccation cracks → high clay, neutral-alkaline pH, high P/K retention
- Red (Alfisols): Reddish, moderate structure → iron-rich, low OM, often nitrogen-deficient
- Alluvial (Entisols/Inceptisols): Pale to medium brown, loose → variable, check region
- Laterite: Bright red-orange, hardened → iron/aluminum-rich, acidic, P fixation high
- Sandy (Entisols, coastal/desert): Pale, single-grain → low OM, nutrients, poor retention
